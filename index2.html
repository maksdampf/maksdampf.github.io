Maya Server

David Burkhardt Maya is a Server designed for Protonet GmbH. Protonets approach to servers is all about accessability. The goal is to enable anybody to run their server Infrastructure from within their office, without needing an IT-Operator to manage it. This led to a different design approach compared to traditional server chassis.

The device is cooled passively, without fans. Its Interface consists only of one button that is surrounded by one status light that can express exceptions, such as updating, backup, rebooting, errors, as colored light animations.

As an all-metal design, manufacturing scales from craftsman level production methods such as cnc-lasercutting and hand-welding for some ten to hundred devices. More efficient cnc-stamping and robot-welding are used when a a few hundred are produced.

Engineering and production ramp has been done by the designer himself. This made it possible to introduce manufacturing tweaks and improve processes, in an iterative fashion, without consulting of other parties. Through these iterations it was possible over time to reduce manufacturing effort by 50% while at the same time improving quality and lead time.

Patent DE 102013010867 B4

David Burkhardt To create a noiseless Server, a fanless cooling method had to be invented. Crucial to the technology involved was paying special attention to the operating temperature range of each individual component.

Heat from the processor, is dissipated in a Heatsink on top of the chassis. This generates an airflow by induced convection, that sucks in cold air from between the harddisks. These Harddisks have the lowest temperature range and are thus positioned at the bottom of the chassis in the cooler intake airflow.

This working principle was benchtested against conventional cooling methods. The Patent was filed in 2013 and granted by DPMA in 08/2016.

Carla 2 Server

David Burkhardt & Tillmann Radmer This Server was designed for Protonet as a successor to their popular Carla Server of 2013.

It is not merely an update to the product line, but a complete technical redesign. All components are modular and can be slided out of the chassis individually. A new requirement for increased reliability and independence in case of a power outage or network breakdown, asked for additional plug-in modules.

Like all Protonet products, Carla 2 is a design for accessability. It features only one button and will quietly fit on an office desk unlike a rackmount server. Its passive heatpipe cooling has been designed to fit newer, more powerful Hardware: 8 core, 16 thread Xeon processor, 128Gb of RAM and 50Terabyte of Harddisk-Storage. Modules for an integrated uninterruptable power supply and a fallback internet connection (LTE modem), can be integrated.

Process Engineering

David Burkhardt One challenge when designing for a startup company is to cope with changing pace in development and manufacturing. What started as a test batch, had to be quickly ramped up to several thousand devices. Enabling this evolution, several new processes were developed. Processes consist of setting up ad-hoc or permanent production lines, creating instruction books, efficient onboarding standards for part-timers, or the design of additional tooling or software.

Product design can play a mayor role in accelerating the production ramp and assuring quality. For example the use of rugged transport cases for subcontractor logistics not only sped up the process, but resulted in no more sub standard parts from damage by handling. It also led to an almost hundred percent decrease in packaging waste during manufacturing logistics.

  • 2009-2016
  • Protonet GmbH

Prototype Jigs

David Burkhardt Design work for a startup company is about speed and iterations.

True to the concept of a minimum viable product (MVP), tooling for prototypes have to be quick, light and cheap. Constructed mostly from 3d printed parts, wood and little use of machined parts, these simple hand-operated jigs can be made in few days of work, sometimes only hours. Compared to outsourced tool engineering, this saves weeks or months in lead time, that are better spent on product iterations.

Originally designed to only make a single prototype, some of these tools can even serve for much longer in volume production.

  • 2012
  • Protonet GmbH

Outdoor Furniture

David Burkhardt This set of outdoor furniture can be made from only two types of recycled wood. The design is inspired by Will Holmans Scrap Table (Guerilla furniture), but using other proportions and simplified materials: generic 4x6 roof lath and 2x10 concrete shuttering board.

The tapered legs are stressed to give necessary stiffness to the thin board. Contrary to Holmans design, the legs are not interconnected by threaded rods. Non-triangulated beams do not add much stability to the space frame, so they were left out. The design is open source.

Spring scissors

David Burkhardt A local scissors manufacturer wanted to launch a new retail brand for scissors. As a original manufacturer, the client already produced scissors for several well known retail brands, but wanted to have a retail marque on their own. The client asked Prof. Glen Oliver Loew of the HfbK, who created a student team to come up with design ideas that have recognition value.

Spring scissors are not new, in fact they are some of the earliest examples of scissors in human history. But since then the concept of a spring scissor, which stays open by itself and closes when pressed, has been reduced to a few niche applications like sheep-shearing.

Not rightly so: Despite weaknesses like less cutting thickness capability, scissor shears also have unique strengths. Having no link at the end of the blades, it is easier to cut along curved shapes.

The design got acknowledgement from the client, but due to unknown processes and materials, was not chosen for manufacturing.

  • Prof. Glen Oliver Loew
  • Scherenmanufaktur Paul GmbH
  • spring steel 1.4310, V2A, Polyurethane
  • lasercut, vacuum moulded
